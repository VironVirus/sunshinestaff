# Sunshine Hotel Staff Portal

## Week 1 Onboarding and Training Plan

### Purpose
This plan is for the first week of onboarding staff into the Sunshine Hotel Staff Portal.
It is designed for support staff or trainers who will introduce the system to:

- Front Office Manager
- Front Office Line Staff
- HouseKeeping Manager
- HouseKeeping Line Staff
- Food and Beverages Manager
- Food and Beverages Line Staff

This week focuses on the live operational parts of the portal that these teams need first:

- Login and navigation
- Staff dashboard and personal information
- Shift visibility and assignment
- Front Office room publishing and breakfast updates
- HouseKeeping cleaned-room updates and out-of-order reporting
- Food and Beverages occupancy and breakfast visibility
- Manager reporting, printing, and download workflow
- Information, announcements, birthdays, and recognition

### Training Goals
By the end of Week 1:

1. Every participant should be able to log in successfully.
2. Every line staff member should know how to view their own dashboard, shifts, surcharges, leave eligibility, and hotel news.
3. Every manager should understand the difference between the `Work`, `Information`, and role-specific operational sections.
4. Front Office managers should be able to publish occupied rooms correctly using the room dropdown flow.
5. HouseKeeping managers should be able to update cleaned rooms and room status in real time.
6. Food and Beverages managers should be able to check live in-house and breakfast figures before service.
7. Each department should complete at least one practical end-to-end exercise successfully.

### Audience Breakdown

#### Managers
Managers should be trained to:

- use operational tabs
- update department work areas
- assign team shifts
- interpret live numbers
- print or download reports where permitted

#### Line Staff
Line staff should be trained to:

- log in and sign out
- view personal details
- check shifts
- review surcharges and leave eligibility
- read news, announcements, birthdays, and recognition
- understand what they must report back to their manager for update in the system

### Week 1 Training Structure
Recommended format:

- `6 training days`
- `1 review and supervised live-use day`
- `2 main sessions per day`
- `1 short practical assessment per day`

Recommended daily timing:

- `09:00 - 10:30` Morning session
- `10:30 - 10:45` Break
- `10:45 - 12:30` Guided practical session
- `13:30 - 15:00` Department-specific role session
- `15:00 - 16:00` Live practice and questions

### Pre-Training Setup Checklist
The support staff should complete this before Day 1:

1. Confirm the live app link is working.
2. Confirm the hotel logo loads on all pages.
3. Confirm each trainee has a registered account.
4. Confirm managers and line staff have the correct department and role.
5. Confirm birthdays are entered for all newly created staff accounts.
6. Confirm at least one printer is connected for report testing.
7. Confirm a PDF download can be opened on at least one training laptop or phone.
8. Prepare a test list of rooms for practice.
9. Prepare one or two test complaints and one test event for demonstration.
10. Prepare a printed attendance sheet for training sign-off.

### Required Materials

- One laptop for the trainer
- One device per trainee if possible
- Internet access
- Printer access for report demonstration
- Staff account list by department
- Sample room-status scenarios
- Sample shift schedule list
- Sample breakfast scenarios

## Week 1 Schedule

### Day 1: General Orientation for All Staff
**Theme:** Understanding the portal and role-based access

**Participants:**

- Front Office Manager
- Front Office Line Staff
- HouseKeeping Manager
- HouseKeeping Line Staff
- Food and Beverages Manager
- Food and Beverages Line Staff

**Morning session objectives:**

- explain why the portal is being used
- explain the difference between manager access and line-staff access
- show the home page, login flow, and dashboard structure
- explain the `Staff Dashboard`, `Work`, and `Information` sections

**Trainer walkthrough:**

1. Open the login page.
2. Show how to sign in.
3. Show how staff names, roles, and departments appear.
4. Show the staff dashboard view for line staff.
5. Show the manager dashboard view.
6. Explain that only managers use the operational update sections.
7. Show where birthdays, staff of the week, staff of the month, and announcements appear.

**Hands-on activity:**

- every trainee logs in
- every trainee confirms their department name
- every trainee checks if their birthday is correct
- every trainee signs out and signs in again

**Assessment for Day 1:**

- each user must log in without help
- each user must point to the section where they will work daily

### Day 2: Front Office Manager and Front Office Line Staff
**Theme:** Room publishing, breakfast control, and occupancy reporting

**Participants:**

- Front Office Manager
- Front Office Line Staff

**Morning session for Front Office Manager:**

- explain the room management workflow
- explain the operational-day logic
- explain why rooms must be selected from floor and room dropdowns
- explain how breakfast count affects hotel reporting

**Manager workflow to teach:**

1. Go to `Work`.
2. Open the `Rooms` section.
3. Select `Floor`.
4. Select `Room`.
5. Select number of days booked.
6. Select whether breakfast is included.
7. Enter the number entitled to breakfast.
8. Publish the room.
9. Release a room when the guest checks out.
10. Print or download the report.

**Live practice scenarios:**

- assign room `104` for `3 days` with `2 breakfast`
- assign one annex room with no breakfast
- release one room
- print the room status report

**Afternoon session for Front Office Line Staff:**

- show the line-staff dashboard only
- explain where to check shift dates
- explain how to see announcements and personal details
- explain what information line staff must pass to the manager for entry

**Line-staff learning points:**

- room updates are done by the manager account
- line staff must report occupancy changes quickly and accurately
- line staff should verify guest breakfast entitlement before passing it for entry

**Assessment for Day 2:**

- manager completes one full room publishing exercise correctly
- line staff explains the correct reporting flow to the manager

### Day 3: HouseKeeping Manager and HouseKeeping Line Staff
**Theme:** Cleaned rooms, room condition, and operational coordination

**Participants:**

- HouseKeeping Manager
- HouseKeeping Line Staff

**Morning session for HouseKeeping Manager:**

- explain how HouseKeeping sees available rooms
- explain how cleaned-room updates affect operational visibility
- explain how out-of-order rooms should be handled
- explain why out-of-order rooms should not remain in available stock

**Manager workflow to teach:**

1. Open `Work`.
2. Open the `Rooms` section for cleaned-room updates.
3. Select floor and room for cleaned rooms.
4. Publish cleaned room updates.
5. Remove a cleaned-room entry if entered in error.
6. Open `Property`.
7. Mark a room as out of order.
8. Enter what needs to be done in the room.

**Live practice scenarios:**

- mark three rooms as cleaned
- remove one incorrect cleaned-room entry
- mark one room out of order
- verify the out-of-order room no longer appears as available

**Afternoon session for HouseKeeping Line Staff:**

- show how to view shifts
- explain how line staff communicate cleaned-room status to the manager
- explain the importance of real-time updates for reception and sales

**Line-staff learning points:**

- cleaned rooms must be reported as soon as they are ready
- room faults must be escalated immediately
- line staff should use the dashboard mainly to check shifts, notices, and personal information

**Assessment for Day 3:**

- manager successfully marks rooms cleaned and one room out of order
- line staff explains the correct escalation route for a room fault

### Day 4: Front Office and HouseKeeping Joint Coordination Day
**Theme:** Real-time room flow between departments

**Participants:**

- Front Office Manager
- HouseKeeping Manager
- selected line staff from both departments

**Objective:**
Build confidence in the shared room workflow across both teams.

**Joint exercise:**

1. Front Office publishes a room as occupied.
2. HouseKeeping confirms the room disappears from cleaned-room availability.
3. HouseKeeping marks a different room as cleaned.
4. Front Office confirms the room is available for allocation.
5. HouseKeeping marks one room as out of order.
6. Front Office confirms that room cannot be assigned.

**Discussion points:**

- what causes room count errors
- how delayed updates affect sales and breakfast planning
- how to verify room status before guest check-in

**Assessment for Day 4:**

- both managers complete the workflow without trainer correction
- both departments agree on the same room status at the end of the drill

### Day 5: Food and Beverages Manager and Food and Beverages Line Staff
**Theme:** Using live occupancy and breakfast data for service readiness

**Participants:**

- Food and Beverages Manager
- Food and Beverages Line Staff

**Morning session for F&B Manager:**

- explain where live in-house figures appear
- explain where breakfast entitlement appears
- explain how these numbers guide food preparation and breakfast staffing
- explain how to monitor announcements and hotel information

**Manager workflow to teach:**

1. Open `Work`.
2. Open the `Rooms` visibility section.
3. Read the `In-house` figure.
4. Read the `Breakfast entitlement` figure.
5. Use the numbers to prepare breakfast planning.
6. Check `Information` for hotel notices and updates.

**Live practice scenarios:**

- compare breakfast entitlement with an expected breakfast setup plan
- identify if the current breakfast number is enough for service preparation
- note any mismatch that should be escalated to Front Office

**Afternoon session for F&B Line Staff:**

- show the line-staff dashboard
- explain how to review shifts, notices, and personal records
- explain how breakfast service staff receive figures from the manager

**Line-staff learning points:**

- the line staff should not guess breakfast numbers
- service preparation must follow the manager-confirmed dashboard figures
- line staff should escalate shortages or unusual breakfast demand quickly

**Assessment for Day 5:**

- F&B manager reads the correct in-house and breakfast values from the app
- line staff explains where breakfast instructions come from

### Day 6: Manager Operations Drill and Team Scheduling
**Theme:** Manager-only control, shift assignment, and reporting discipline

**Participants:**

- Front Office Manager
- HouseKeeping Manager
- Food and Beverages Manager

**Objectives:**

- teach managers to assign shifts
- review daily reporting discipline
- test operational coordination

**Manager tasks to practice:**

1. Open the `Team` section.
2. Assign shifts to team members.
3. Remove an incorrect shift.
4. Check that staff can later see the shift on their personal dashboard.
5. Review information and announcements.
6. Review birthdays and staff recognition for staff communication.

**Cross-department drill:**

- Front Office publishes final occupancy
- HouseKeeping publishes final cleaned rooms
- Food and Beverages reads the final breakfast figure
- managers confirm they all see matching live data

**Assessment for Day 6:**

- each manager assigns at least one shift
- each manager completes one department-specific task without help

### Day 7: Supervised Live Use and Sign-Off
**Theme:** Real operation with trainer observation

**Participants:**

- all Week 1 trainees

**Plan for the day:**

- trainer observes staff using the system in real time
- trainer only steps in when absolutely necessary
- each department performs one real or mock operational update

**Required sign-off actions:**

- Front Office Manager publishes one real or mock occupied room
- HouseKeeping Manager publishes one cleaned room or room-status change
- Food and Beverages Manager reads the breakfast count correctly
- one line staff member from each department checks and confirms their shift

**End-of-week review meeting:**

- what was easy
- what was confusing
- what requires retraining
- what data-entry rules should be reinforced

## Role-Based Quick Reference

### Front Office Manager
Primary training points:

- assign occupied rooms using `Floor -> Room -> Days -> Breakfast -> Breakfast count`
- release rooms correctly
- understand that wrong room entry affects room sales, breakfast, and reporting
- use print and PDF report outputs

### Front Office Line Staff
Primary training points:

- check personal dashboard and shifts
- communicate guest movement and breakfast entitlement to the manager quickly
- confirm room numbers before escalation

### HouseKeeping Manager
Primary training points:

- publish cleaned rooms
- manage out-of-order room updates
- keep room status aligned with actual physical room condition

### HouseKeeping Line Staff
Primary training points:

- report cleaned rooms immediately
- escalate damage or faults quickly
- check shifts and announcements daily

### Food and Beverages Manager
Primary training points:

- use live in-house and breakfast numbers to plan service
- compare breakfast figures before service opens
- coordinate with Front Office when numbers change

### Food and Beverages Line Staff
Primary training points:

- check shifts and internal communication
- receive confirmed breakfast numbers from the manager
- follow updated service expectations

## Daily Trainer Checklist
The support staff or trainer should do this every day:

1. Open the app 15 minutes before training.
2. Confirm internet, login, and dashboard access.
3. Confirm the right staff are in the room.
4. Record attendance.
5. Demonstrate before asking trainees to perform tasks.
6. Observe each participant doing the task alone.
7. Record common mistakes.
8. Repeat high-risk tasks before ending the session.

## Common Mistakes to Watch For

- selecting the wrong room floor before choosing a room
- entering breakfast count when breakfast is not included
- forgetting to release checked-out rooms
- marking a room cleaned before it is physically ready
- failing to report out-of-order rooms immediately
- relying on verbal numbers instead of confirming the dashboard
- line staff expecting to see manager-only update sections

## Success Criteria for Week 1
Week 1 is successful if:

1. All trainees can log in and identify their correct dashboard view.
2. All managers can perform their core task without assistance.
3. Line staff understand what they can view and what must be escalated.
4. Front Office, HouseKeeping, and Food and Beverages can explain how their data connects.
5. At least one full cross-department exercise is completed successfully.

## Recommended Week 2 Follow-Up
After this week, the next training phase should cover:

- Night Duty
- Maintenance
- Store
- HR
- Executive users
- report review and escalation standards
- live operational governance and audit checks
